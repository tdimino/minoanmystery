# Canaanite Absorption: How Yahweh Inherited the Baalim

## Overview

Lewis Bayles Paton's 1914 study "Canaanite Influence on the Religion of Israel" documents the mechanism by which foreign religious traditions entered Yahwism: **absorption**. Israel did not exterminate Canaanite religion but gradually absorbed its deities, holy places, sacred traditions, rituals, and festivals. The local *bē'ālîm* (lords, patron gods) were first identified with Yahweh, then their independent existence was forgotten. Personal names like **Ba'al-Yah** ("Yahweh is Ba'al") prove that Israelites explicitly equated their god with Canaanite numina. Sacred sites persisted from Canaanite through Islamic times, demonstrating remarkable religious continuity beneath surface theological changes.

This absorption mechanism is essential for understanding how Cretan religious traditions could enter Israelite religion via the Cherethites/Philistines. The same process that absorbed Canaanite Dagon absorbed the Cretan Zeus whom the Philistines identified with Dagon. The mechanism was not conversion but **recognition**: newcomers identified their ancestral deity in the god of the land they settled.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Gradual Conquest, Not Extermination

**Paton** (1914):
> "The older sources show, accordingly, that the conquest of Canaan by Israel was a process that extended over several centuries. The aborigines were not exterminated, but certain Hebrew clans forced their way into the land, and occupied the rural districts, while the walled cities remained, for the most part, in the hands of the Canaanites."

**The resulting population**:
> "For a long while there was hostility between the two races; but gradually this ceased, and a process of amalgamation began... In process of time, through conquest, treaty, or inter-marriage, Canaanites and Hebrews were fused into one people."

> "The Israel of David's day was not the lineal descendant of the nation that entered Canaan under Moses and Joshua, but was a hybrid race composed partly of Israelites and partly of Canaanites."

**Implication**: The religious traditions of Canaan were not destroyed but inherited. Any group settling in the land underwent the same process of religious fusion.

### 2. Adoption of Language and Civilization

**Paton on cultural absorption**:
> "When the Hebrews entered Canaan they were rude nomads of the desert, while the Canaanites had attained a high civilization... It is probable even that the newcomers adopted the language of the Canaanites instead of the Aramaic dialect that they spoke originally. The language that we call Hebrew is the language of the glosses to the Tell el-Amarna Letters, and Isa. 19:18 calls it 'the tongue of Canaan.'"

**Religion followed civilization**:
> "With this adoption of the civilization of Canaan there must have come adoption of the gods of Canaan. Agriculture could not be carried on without observing the ceremonies that accompanied the planting of the seed and the reaping of the harvest."

The Israelites adopted Canaanite language, Canaanite agricultural practice, and Canaanite agricultural religion. The same would apply to any group—including the Philistines—settling in the land.

### 3. The Bē'ālîm Identified with Yahweh

**The ubiquity of local gods** (Paton):
> "There must have been innumerable other *bē'ālîm* whose places of worship are not recorded, since, according to Jer. 2:28; 3:6, they were as numerous as the towns, and were worshiped on every high hill and under every green tree."

**The identification process**:
> "At first they were regarded as different gods from Yahweh; but *ba'al*, 'proprietor,' was a generic name that might also be applied to him, and little by little they were identified with him."

**Theophoric name evidence**:
> "In such names as *Jerub-ba'al*, 'the *ba'al* contends'; *Ish-ba'al*, 'man of *ba'al*'; *Ba'al-yada'*, 'the *ba'al* knows'; *Ba'al-hanan*, 'the *ba'al* is gracious,' *ba'al* is certainly a title of Yahweh. In one case, **Ba'al-Yah**, 'Yahweh is the *ba'al*,' the identity of the *ba'al* with Yahweh is asserted."

**Popular understanding** (Paton):
> "In popular conception in the time of Hosea the *bē'ālîm* were not foreign gods but local Yahwehs."

The mechanism is explicit: local deities were not rejected but **relabeled** as manifestations of Yahweh.

### 4. Euhemerization: Gods Become Ancestors

**Paton on the transformation**:
> "These patron-gods of ancient clans were not identified by the Hebrews with Yahweh, and their names did not become his epithets; but they were degraded to the rank of human ancestors of the clans that were named after them. Their ancient shrines were transformed into graves, and the homage that was still paid them was regarded as reverence for forefathers."

**Place-name evidence**:
> "Such names as *Jabne-el*, *Jezre-el*, etc., are properly names of persons. Their use as place-names can be explained only by elipsis of *bêth*, 'house of,'... All of these names, accordingly, point to a cult of assumed ancestors at their supposed places of burial."

**The pattern**:
```
Divine name (god) → Personal name (ancestor) → Place name (burial site)
   Yisra-el       →      Israel          →     Beth-el
```

**Application to Minos**: If Canaanite tribal gods became "ancestors," the same process could work in reverse at Gaza: Minos the ancestor-king was elevated (or restored) to divine status. The euhemerization that reduced gods to men could be reversed for men who had always been gods.

### 5. Holy Places Appropriated

**Paton on sacred site continuity**:
> "In every place where a god was believed to manifest himself in a special way the Canaanites established a sanctuary... Over a hundred places in Canaan are attested as holy by the meaning of their names, by the rites that were practiced there, or by the evidence of archaeology."

> "When Yahweh triumphed over the *bē'ālîm* and other *ēlōhîm* of Canaan these holy places were appropriated by him. They were the 'high places' in which Israel worshiped Yahweh without opposition until the period of the great prophets."

**Persistence across millennia**:
> "Deuteronomy was unable, however, to destroy the reverence for these places. Their sanctity has lasted without interruption down to modern times. In spite of all the efforts of Judaism, Christianity, and Mohammedanism, one may still say with the author of Kings, 'Nevertheless the high places are not taken away, the people still sacrifice and burn incense in the high places.'"

**The implication**: Sacred geography survived every theological revolution. The same caves, mountains, and trees remained holy regardless of which god was officially worshipped there.

### 6. Sacred Objects Transferred

**Maṣṣēbhōth (standing stones)**:
> "An essential part of the equipment of a high place was a *maṣṣēbhā* or 'standing stone,' which constituted the *bĕth-ēl*, or 'abode of deity,' and served in primitive times at once as temple, image, and altar."

> "That Yahweh was believed actually to inhabit them is shown by the facts that the one set up by Jacob was called *Beth-ēl*, 'dwelling of God,' or *El-bêth-ēl*, 'God of the dwelling of God.'"

**Āshērīm (sacred poles/trees)**:
> "The *āshērā*, or sacred post, was also an indispensable accessory of Canaanite high places... The Canaanite *āshērîm* were also appropriated by Yahweh after the conquest of the land. Both in Samaria and in Jerusalem they stood in his temples."

The physical apparatus of Canaanite worship became the physical apparatus of Yahweh worship.

### 7. The Mechanism Applied to Cretan Traditions

**How Cretan Zeus entered Yahwism**:

The same absorption process that incorporated Canaanite *bē'ālîm* would have incorporated Philistine/Cretan traditions:

1. **Philistines settle Gaza** (c. 1175 BCE): Bring Cretan Zeus (Marnas) traditions
2. **Philistines adopt Dagon**: Identify their Cretan Zeus with local Canaanite grain-god
3. **Cherethites serve David** (c. 1000 BCE): Cretan-Canaanite religious hybrid enters Israelite court
4. **Absorption into Yahwism**: The same process that identified Canaanite *ba'al* with Yahweh identified Cretan Zeus/Dagon/Marnas with Yahweh
5. **The YHW coin** (c. 400 BCE): Documents the identification—YHWH depicted as Zeus at Gaza

**The pattern**:
```
Cretan Zeus → Dagon (Canaanite identification) → Ba'al (generic title) → Yahweh
```

### 8. Agricultural Festivals from Canaan

**Paton on the agricultural calendar**:
> "Three pilgrimage feasts yearly seem to have been a feature of the primitive Mosaic religion (Exod. 23:14, 17; 34:23), but after the occupation of Canaan these were transformed into agricultural festivals, the 'days of the *bē'ālîm*,' as Hosea calls them (2:13)."

**Unleavened Bread**:
> "The Feast of Unleavened Bread celebrated the early barley-harvest... A feast of this sort evidently cannot have originated in the desert; it is part of the agricultural ritual of the land of Canaan."

**Weeks/Pentecost**:
> "The agricultural character of this festival [Feast of Weeks] is obvious: it must have been of Canaanite rather than of primitive Hebrew origin."

**The implication**: Israel's sacred calendar derives from Canaanite agricultural religion. If the Cretan Zeus (Marnas) was a grain-god like Dagon, his festivals would have merged into this same agricultural cycle.

### 9. Yahweh's Victory Through Absorption

**Paton's summary**:
> "Yahweh triumphed over the *bē'ālîm* and the other gods of the land by absorbing them. All their attributes, activities, sacred objects, holy places, altars, sacred traditions, ritual, and feasts were appropriated by him; and the result was that his religion was mixed with all sorts of alien elements, just as Christianity in its first centuries was mixed with Greco-Roman ideas."

**Not destruction but incorporation**: Yahweh did not defeat the *bē'ālîm* by eliminating them but by becoming them. Every local deity became a face of Yahweh. The Cretan Zeus of Gaza—known as Marnas, identified with Dagon, worshipped by David's Cherethites—was absorbed into Yahweh along with every other Canaanite *ba'al*.
