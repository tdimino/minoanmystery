## Divine Names

| Minoan | Semitic | Identity | Text References |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| *a-du* | אַדּוּ / הַדּוּ | Addu (= Baal) | 85:a:1; 86:a:4; 88:1; 92:1; 95:b:1; 99:a:1; 133:1 |
| *a-ka-ru* | אֻגָרוּ | Ugaru (Baal's messenger) | 2:1; 86:a:1, b:1 |
| *ku-pa-na-tu-na* | — | divine name or epithet | I,13:b:1 |
| *pu-ra* | בֹּעַל | Bōl (< Baal) | 28:a:3; 116:2 |
| *ti-ni-ta* | תִּנִּית | Tinit (goddess) | 27:1 |
| *ya-mu* | יַמּוּ | Yamm (sea-god) | 28:a:4, b:1 |

**Note** (§163 n.89): *a-du* (Baal) is the most frequently mentioned deity in Linear A texts. *pu-ra* (Bōl) receives offerings paralleling those to Yamm—the pair correspond to Zeus and Poseidon.
